Gerund (V + ing)

1. After adjectives

It’s important not to be late

It’s nice to be back home

1. As a noun

Eating outside in the summer makes me feel good

Happiness is getting up early and not going to work

Walking is a good form of exercise 

2. To express purpose (to explain why you do/did something)

I wore a suit to make a good impression

She went to the bank to get some money

2. After prepositions

I’m tired of going to work  by bus every morning

I’m thinking of buying a new car

Jim left without saying goodbye

3. After question words (where, when, what, etc.)

I don’t know where to go and what to do

I don’t know how to answer this question

!!! These verbs can be followed either by infinitive or gerund begin / continue / like / prefer / start

  1. It was necessary - had to
  2. It was possible / It was permitted  - could, was / were allowed to
  3. It wasn’t necessary - didn’t have to
  4. It wasn’t permitted / it was prohibited - couldn’t, wasn’t / weren’t allowed to
  5. It’s obligatory. It’s law or rule - must / have to
  6. It’s not necessary. You can do it if you want - don’t have to
  7. It’s forbidden to do smth - mustn’t
  8. It’s allowed or possible- - can
  9. It’s a good (bad) idea to do it / advice or recommendation - should / shouldn’t
  10. It’s not allowed - can’t

                  Make, let, allow

1.make smb do smth

Our teacher makes us do the homework

  1. let smb do smth

Semco lets us take company’s cars

  1. – (not) allow smb to do smth

They usually allow us to stay overnight

They don’t allow us to smoke in the flat

  • (not) allow + V ing

They don’t allow smoking in the flat
